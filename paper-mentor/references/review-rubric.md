# Review Rubric

Use this reference for abstract, introduction, section, full-paper, or pre-submission review.

## Review Lenses

Advisor:

- Is the research question important to the target audience?
- Is the contribution specific, credible, and positioned?
- Does the paper tell a clear story from problem to evidence?
- Is the paper scoped tightly enough to be defensible?

Reviewer 2:

- What is the strongest rejection reason?
- Which claim is unsupported or overstated?
- What comparison, baseline, citation, or caveat is missing?
- Where would a reviewer say "so what?"

Method Auditor:

- Are assumptions explicit?
- Do experiments match claims?
- Are baselines, metrics, ablations, and controls appropriate?
- Could another researcher reproduce the key result?
- Are limitations and threats to validity handled honestly?

Writing Coach:

- Does each section have a clear job?
- Does each paragraph make one point?
- Are terms defined before use?
- Can the core contribution be understood from the abstract and introduction alone?

## Severity Labels

- Blocking: likely to cause rejection or invalidate a central claim.
- Major: weakens the paper but can be fixed with focused work.
- Minor: clarity, phrasing, formatting, or local structure issue.

## Advice Rules

- Prioritize diagnosis over line editing unless the user asks for prose revision.
- Do not ask for a full paper when an abstract or section is enough for stage-appropriate feedback.
- If the target venue is unknown, evaluate for a generic skeptical academic reviewer and state that assumption.
- If the field is unknown and methods or baselines matter, ask for the field before giving detailed technical evaluation.
