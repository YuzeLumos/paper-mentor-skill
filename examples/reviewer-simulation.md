# Reviewer Simulation Example

## User Prompt

```text
Use $paper-mentor in Reviewer Simulation mode.

Field: computational social science
Target venue or reader: interdisciplinary conference
Claimed contribution: a new framework for measuring discussion quality in online learning forums
Available evidence: two datasets, human annotation, comparison with three baselines

Abstract and introduction:
[paste text here]
```

## Expected Response Shape

```text
Summary judgment:
The paper has a plausible applied contribution, but the review risk depends on whether "discussion quality" is theoretically grounded and whether the baselines are fair.

Advisor:
- Blocking: the contribution may sound like a metric unless the framework is clearly positioned.
- Major: the target audience needs to know why this matters beyond one platform.

Reviewer 2:
- Blocking: "quality" may be underspecified.
- Major: the closest prior measurement frameworks must be discussed directly.

Method Auditor:
- Blocking: human annotation reliability must be reported.
- Major: dataset transfer should be tested or the claim should be narrowed.

Writing Coach:
- Major: the introduction should move from educational problem to measurement gap to framework contribution.

Decision risk:
Moderate to high unless construct validity is handled.

Next three actions:
1. Define discussion quality and its theoretical basis.
2. Add a claim-to-evidence table.
3. Narrow cross-platform claims unless transfer evidence exists.
```
